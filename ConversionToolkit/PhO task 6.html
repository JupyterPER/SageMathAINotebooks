6. Collision in a Track

For the experiment with ball motion, a track consisting of two parts was used. An arc $o_1$ with radius $R_1$ connects at the lowest point to arc $o_2$ with radius $R_2$, which ends at its highest point, Fig. Dâ€“5.

Experiment description:

At the highest point of track $o_1$ at height $R_1$ above the lowest point of the track, we release ball $G_1$ with mass $m_1$, which slides down to the lowest point, where it collides with a second equally sized ball $G_2$ with mass $m_2$, which then continues along arc $o_2$.

Assumptions:

  1. Friction between balls and tracks is neglected.
  2. Ball dimensions are very small compared to arc radii.
  3. The collision of balls is perfectly elastic.

---

Problem assignments:

a) Determine the ratio $k = \frac{R_1}{R_2}$ of arc radii so that ball $G_2$ passes through the highest point of track $o_2$.

b) What maximum height $h_{m1}$ does ball $G_1$ reach after collision and to what maximum height $h_{m2}$ does ball $G_2$ rise, if $R_1 = 2R_2$ in cases of mass ratio $p = \frac{m_2}{m_1}$:

  • $p_1 = 0.5$
  • $p_2 = 2.0$

$h_{m2} = \dfrac{32}{9} R_2 \approx 3.55 R_2$ clearly indicates that ball 2 has sufficient mechanical energy to pass through the top of the second track (height $h_{2m}=2R_2$).

Let's divide the corresponding energy $E_{total} = m_2 g \dfrac{32}{9} R_2$ into two components:

  • potential energy at height $E_p = m_2 g 2R_2$
  • remaining kinetic energy $E_k = \frac{1}{2} \; m_2 v_{22}^2$

By calculating velocity $v_{22}$ - the velocity with which the ball would pass through the highest point of the smaller track, we will then be able to compare centrifugal and gravitational forces. As the centrifugal force is greater than gravitational force, the ball will definitely be able to reach height $2R_2$, while having some extra kinetic energy (after leaving the upper point of the track it would fly further to the right, but not to a greater height).
